Realistic Research: University, Web-based, and Local Resources

This lesson is designed to help students beginning research in their first semester acquire effective research skills. Learn strategies to access resources through the following areas:

Getting Started

Learn how to access IIT library services and navigate the IIT library website.

Using myIIT allows a proxy connection, enabling access to most resources without reentering login information.

Research Timelines

Formulate realistic timelines for your research projects, considering resource availability and deadlines.

Being Realistic

Don't assume you can read an entire book in one day. Set realistic goals to avoid overloading yourself.

Consider factors like the time to obtain, read, and integrate resources into your project.

Requesting Resources

Instructions on how to request articles and books through I-Share and MyILL.

Local Libraries

Explore options for accessing resources at local libraries, such as the Chicago Public Library and I-Share affiliated libraries.

Being Realistic

Check library hours and policies before making a trip to avoid wasted time.

Finding Resources on the Web

Overview of web resources like Google Scholar, Google Books, and Web of Science for research.

Being Realistic

If you're stuck, try searching for special issues or edited volumes related to your topic.

Quiz

Test your understanding of the Research Module. Select the best answer for each question.

1. Which service allows proxy server access to library resources?


2. Why is it important to create a realistic research timeline?


3. Which tool can help you find books and articles at other libraries?


4. Which online platform is recommended for finding academic articles?
